U.S. Short-term Rental Regulations Impact Analysis (2010-2024)

This analysis, prepared by Scott Glasgow (FinMango) for Chen Zhang, PhD (Iowa State University), examines Google search trends in the United States related to short-term rental regulations, housing affordability, and labor markets over a 15-year period.

Understanding the Data

The data used in this analysis represents Google search query volumes for various terms related to short-term rentals in the United States from 2010 to 2024. Here's what you need to know:

  • Data Source: Google Trends API.
  • Data Format: The values represent the probability of a search term multiplied by 10 million.
  • Interpretation: Higher values indicate greater search interest for a particular term relative to all terms searched in the U.S. during that time period.
  • Time Range: The data covers the period from 2010 to 2024, allowing us to observe long-term trends in public interest regarding these topics.

Search Interest: Housing Affordability

This chart shows the trends in search interest for affordable housing from 2010 to 2024. Key observations:

  • Search interest in affordable housing has generally increased over the 15-year period, indicating growing public concern or awareness.
  • Note that this trend may be influenced by overall increases in housing costs and other economic factors beyond short-term rental regulations.

Search Interest: Short-term Rental Regulations

This chart illustrates the trends in search interest for short-term rental laws and Airbnb regulations from 2010 to 2024. Key observations:

  • Interest in short-term rental laws and Airbnb regulations began to appear in searches around 2015, with significant increases after 2020.
  • The rise in searches for regulatory terms may reflect increasing public awareness of policy changes or debates surrounding short-term rentals.

Search Interest: Labor Market

This chart illustrates the trends in search interest for hospitality jobs and cleaning services from 2010 to 2024. Key observations:

  • Search interest in cleaning services has shown a general increase over the 15-year period, potentially indicating growing demand or awareness of these services.
  • Interest in hospitality jobs has fluctuated, with a notable spike in 2020 followed by a sharp decline, possibly reflecting job market changes during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • These trends may be influenced by factors beyond short-term rental regulations, such as overall economic conditions and public health events.
